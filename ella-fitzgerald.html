Ella Fitzgerald

Known for:

A music genre is named after her.
So good even racists liked her.
Insane vocal range

Chosen Quote:

"Just don’t give up trying to do what you really want to do. Where there is love and inspiration, I don’t think you can go wrong."

In the Coltrane section we discussed Bebop , the side of Jazz where the most difficult variations lie. Well, Bebop was named after Ella Fitzgerald.

Listen to this version of Blue Skies. She begins with a scat solo.

Notice her preference for the 'B' sounds, particularly Bee's and Bahp's. That's where the name comes from.

You've probably seen the meme. The prestigious Mocambo wouldn't let Fitzgerald sing there due to segregation.

In comes Marilyn Monroe and tells the people in charge that if they let Fitzgerald sing at the club, Monroe would be there for every performance. And wherever Monroe partied, the press followed in droves.

Not only did Fitzgerald become a regular in the Mocambo, she never had to play small jaz venues for dimes on the dollar.

Fitzgerald's vocal range was three octaves and change. Very, very few musical pieces are written for such ranges because very few people would be able to perform them.

However, there is a 7 minute gem where Fitzgerald playfully performs with the entirety of her range, from her lowest notes to the operatic highest.

It's called How High the Moon, performed live in Berlin in '62.

Louis Armstrong(center) once impressed a white composer/critic so much, he wrote about Armstrong (paraphrasing), that a man who could play the 'horns' like that had to have a soul. He was being inspired by angels.

Duke Ellington's(right) first name wasn't Duke. He was nicknamed that way given his elegant mannerisms and dressing style. Ellington wanted black people to be considered respected members of the community , and was trying to set the example .

I believe Jazz was powerful enough to even influence racism in the 'States. And like Hugh Laurie said, "America's greatest gift to the world is Jazz".
