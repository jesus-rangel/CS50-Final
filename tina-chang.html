Ok, she's cute. Let's face it.

So is it just my hormones talking? Hot, smart, sensitive girl...

Well, ironically, and oh so powerfully, The Poem is about Woman Objectified. Her as a sex object.

It is about the emptiness and, admittedly, according to her, the vicious pleasure, the power that comes with being beautiful and wanted.

The Poem is called Duality.

It is a brutal, gritty (no loftiness) take on a woman who has embraced her power over men, or man, it doesn't matter to her. If you read it, notice she speaks about one man in particular, but doesn't deign give him a name.

But in romance, where two people should be equals, too often one looks up to the other... and the other one is looking down, even if so high she can touch the clouds...

She speaks of herself as if being somehow hunted by time...

  • Is this about her youth, and her sensuality having an expiration date?
  • Is it about the fear of ending up alone after all?
  • About how she ended up herself objectifying men, and how her comeuppance is inevitable?

I believe all of the above are fascinating, and speak deeply to the loneliness, the anxiety of our times.
