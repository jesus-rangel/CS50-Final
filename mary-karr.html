Mary Karr

Known for:

Writing lyrics for people like Nora Jones.
Writing the most painful poetry...
... And also self-help books (like whut?!)

Chosen Quote:

"Sure the world breeds monsters, but kindness grows just as wild..."
From: The Liar's Club

When I read “A dysfunctional family is any family with more than one person in it" , from a woman who describes her youth as her journey "from black-belt sinner and lifelong agnostic to unlikely Catholic,” and she ends the interview with “Even the best of us are at least part-time bastards.”

... Something tells me this woman is a badass

The interviewer talks about her abused childhood, how she's sweetly but strongly Texan, how she goes hermit-mode when writing, eating pistachoes all day, "oh and, by the way, she curses like a sailor."

There I think to myself I've found my spirit animal in poetry.

Karr may not be known to general audiences, but she has impressed people in the literary world all her life.

She suffered an abusive relationship with the legendary poet David Foster Wallace, who had a mild case of "couldn't stop thinking about her", to the point where he tattooed her name on his body.

She became a New York Times bestselling author after the release of her first memoir, The Liar's Club.

But I like her for her painfully self-violent, humorous, yet concise, one-hit-KO hitting poetry. Check out Karr's My New Diet , if you like.

One reason Karr may be so elusive and hermit-like, is the suffering she describes in her memoirs:

  • Being twice abused by pedophiles
  • Her mother trying to murder her with a butcher knife
  • David Foster Wallace throwing a kitchen table at her

And in her writings, you feel the bottomless darkness she has experienced, but she nourishes you with her feminine strength necessary to survive.

That strength some people have to survive the darkest, most cruel machinations of fate. I don't know where it comes from, and I envy the people who have it.

I do have a clue, though: I think it may come from unrelenting optimism, and a sense of humor.

Near the end of the interview...

Interviewer: "You talk about writer's block. Could you explain what that sounds like in your head?"

Karr: "SH*T! F*CK! DON'T! STOP! F*CK! DON'T! F*CK! SH*T! STOP!"
