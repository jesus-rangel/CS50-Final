Joe Frazier

Known for:

Knocking Ali out in his prime.
Among top 10 heavyweights in history.
Willing to die in the ring.

Chosen Quote:

"Ali kept calling me ugly, but (...) I have 11 babies, somebody thought I was cute."

Joe Frazier was born looking so intimidating that, in school, kids would pay him pennies to walk along with him so bullies would be warded away.

Still, later in his life, coach Eddie Futch considered him basically a chihuahua in the heavyweight division, and had to develop an unorthodox fighting style for his short size.

Frazier's height? 6 and a half inches. Go cry about Tinder girls now, you millenial hipster boy.

That man in the picture was Miguel Angel Castellini, a boxing ex-world champion from Argentina .

I trained under him after I had been bullied in school and had attempted suicide. But then luckily I found combat sports , a much more constructive form of self-destruction.

I'm 5'5. The first time Castellini saw me sparring, my opponent was 6'3. I got destroyed.

Castellini, old, with brain damage, but the eye of a champ still intact, told me my stance was too wide for chasing such tall opponents. That I needed a footwork like Frazier's.

So off I went to study some boxing history, and have my life changed forever.

Frazier was told, like Tyson, he was too short to ever be champion. His reaction made Chihuahuas proud all over the world.

Frazier and his coach acknowledged the height disadvantage, then developed such a hyper-aggressive (Chihuahuas) , anti-orthodox style, that Frazier would advance moving his back foot first.

This would keep Frazier's feet close to each other, always ready to spring forward viciously on his retreating victims. (Like Chihuahas).

* Photo: Chihuahua reacting to Ali vs Frazier (1971) colorized.

Frazier became the first boxer to defeat Ali, and the only boxer to defeat Ali before his health problems began to show.

The last round of their second rematch is referred to as "boxing's lost round." Frazier's coach threw in the towel fearing Frazier would die . "I don't care, boss. I want him". Frazier protested. Ali ended up winning the series, but experts agree nobody knows what would have happened had the towel not been thrown.

Ali, in a rare display of admiration towards Frazier, admitted he felt so close to dying, that he joked between rounds to his coach, "Am I dead yet? I'm pretty sure that ugly gorilla just killed me."

As for me, being 5'5 means that, when I'm in the ring, the size of my opponents is reminiscent of my school bullies. But the adult me has hours of studying Frazier, Tyson and a huge etc. after meeting Castellini.*

It means The adult me can hold the scared little inner child in me, as the round is about to start, and tell him "I've got this. He's not the only wolf in this den. I'll keep you safe."
