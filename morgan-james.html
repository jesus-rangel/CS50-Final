Morgan James

Known for:

Doing a Guns 'N Roses guitar solo with her voice
Impressing Prince by covering one of his songs.
Juilliard opened an extra soprano spot just for her.

Chosen Quote:

"I'd rather make music than being famous". ~ Morgan James, to studios who wanted to change her style.

Juilliard graduate with experience in opera, Broadway musicals, as well as jazz ensembles, not to mention she looks like a blonde from a noir film, this woman just has it all for mainstream success.

However, several studios have approached her to help her "tune" her style and "guide" her to achieve the fame she deserves. Her reply? "No thanks, I sing what I want".

James has been adamant on singing only what touches her deeply , regardless of whether it will sell or not. Aretha Franklin, Nina Simone, Chaka Khan she counts among her inspirations.

Perhaps because she sings from such an honest place in her heart, the youtube comments section's consensus considers her "a force of nature" , "the world's best living singer" and "perfection on the highest level".

Regarding how she achieves those performances live, well, I believe we only need one quote from her: "I sing as if I'm never going to sing ever again".

Somehow, James can achieve the warm low notes of a mezzo-soprano , which would normally be Christina Aguilera's comfort zone, like in her cover of Maroon 5's Maps with the PMJ.

At the same time, she can sing the soprano climax in The Phantom of the Opera.

I don't understand how her vocal chords can do that anymore than I can understand why people listen to Katy Perry when this stuff is online. #SomeoneHadToSayIt

James was initially rejected by Juilliard. Her wiki succinctly states she convinced the head of admissions to open an extra soprano spot just for her.

Between Juilliard and Broadway, there was a 7-year gap where she worked four to five jobs to make ends meet.

Even now you can usually find James' most impressive performances recorded with a cell phone from the audience.

She is still an independent artist attached to no label. Here's hoping she finds the recognition she deserves.
