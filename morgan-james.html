Morgan James

Known for:

Doing Sweet Child Of Mine's guitar solo with her voice
Impressing Prince by covering one of his songs.
Juilliard opened an extra soprano spot just for her.

Chosen Quote:

"I'd rather make music than being famous". ~ Morgan James, to studios who wanted to change her style.

Juilliard graduate with experience in opera, Broadway musicals, as well as jazz ensembles, not to mention this blonde looks like a goddess from a noir film. She just has it all for success.

Several studios have approached her to help her tune her style to achieve the fame she deserves. Her reply? "No thanks, I sing what I want."

James has been adamant on singing only what touches her deeply, regardless of whether it will sell or not. Aretha Franklin, Nina Simone, Shakha Khan she counts among her inspirations.

Perhaps because she sings from such an honest place in her heart, audience members label her "a force of nature", "the world's best living singer" and "perfection on the highest level".

Regarding how she achieves those performances live, well, I believe we only need one quote from her: "I sing as if I'm never going to sing ever again."

Somehow, James can achieve the warm low notes of a mezzo-soprano, which would normally be Christina Aguilera's comfort zone. One example is her cover of Maroon 5's Maps with the PMJ.

At the same time, she can comfortably sing the Soprano climax in The Phantom of the Opera.

I don't understand how her vocal chords can do that anymore than I can understand why people listen to Katy Perry when this stuff is online.

Louis Armstrong (center) once impressed a white composer/critic so much, he wrote about Armstrong, paraphrasing, that a man who could play the 'horns' like that had to have a soul. He was being inspired by angels.

Duke Ellington's (right) first name wasn't Duke. He was nicknamed that way given his elegance and dressing style. Ellington wanted black people to be no longer considered thugs. Sadly, if only he saw rappers today.

I believe Jazz was powerful enough to even influence racism in the 'States. And like Hugh Laurie said, "America's greatest gift to the world is Jazz"
